Circular Economy & Green Entrepreneurship

A circular economy keeps resources in use through repair, reuse, and recycling. Green entrepreneurship brings new business ideas that solve environmental problems while creating value.

Core principles

  • Design out waste and pollution
  • Keep products and materials in use
  • Regenerate natural systems

Examples

  • Upcycling workshops & student repair clubs
  • Refill stations for school supplies
  • Startups using recycled materials or solar power

Green Entrepreneurship

Green entrepreneurs turn circular ideas into sustainable ventures — think compost services, refill shops, repair cafes, and recycled-product startups. Start small, measure impact, and scale with community partners.

Starter checklist

  1. Define the problem & target users
  2. Prototype with low-cost materials
  3. Measure materials saved or emissions avoided
  4. Partner with local groups to scale

Innovations & Approaches

Simple tech and design ideas help implement circular systems: QR-based material tracking, design-for-disassembly, local maker spaces, and community material banks.

Resources

Use these starting points: material flow worksheets, project poster templates, low-cost prototyping guides, and lists of circular project ideas.
